DishInk Inventory connects directly to every order channel — POS, Waiter App, and QR ordering — so your stock updates automatically on every sale. Build recipes with exact ingredient quantities, link them to your menu items, and let the system handle deductions, food cost calculations, low-stock alerts, and automated unavailability blocking. No spreadsheets, no manual counting between services.

## Real-Time Stock Tracking

See exactly what you have in stock at any moment. Every item displays its current quantity alongside a full movement history — how much has been consumed, restocked, or wasted. Low-stock alerts fire when an item approaches your set threshold, giving your team time to reorder before service suffers.

- Current stock visibility across all items
- Full stock movement history per ingredient
- Low-stock alerts at configurable thresholds
- Quantity consumed, restocked, and wasted — all tracked
- Usage history for trend spotting and ordering decisions

## Recipe Management

Build recipes with specific ingredients and exact quantities — grams, millilitres, or units — and link each recipe directly to a menu item. When that item sells from any channel, DishInk automatically deducts every ingredient in the recipe from stock. No manual counting, no spreadsheets, no end-of-day guesswork.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create or select an ingredient">
    Add each ingredient to your inventory with its unit of measure (g, ml, units) and current stock level.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the recipe">
    Open the Recipe Builder, add ingredients, and enter the exact quantity used per serving.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link to a menu item">
    Attach the recipe to the corresponding menu item. Every sale of that item triggers automatic deductions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Recipes support multiple ingredients per item and reusable ingredient entries — so updating a shared ingredient (like a base sauce) updates every recipe that uses it.

## Automatic Deductions

Every time a product is sold through the POS or Waiter App, DishInk fires recipe-based deductions against your stock in real time. You never need to manually adjust inventory after a service — the system keeps stock levels accurate as orders flow through.

- POS sales trigger immediate ingredient deductions
- Waiter App orders deduct identically via the same recipe logic
- Stock levels update in real time with no manual step
- Every deduction is logged with the order reference and timestamp

## Food Cost & Margins

DishInk calculates food costs automatically from your recipes and current ingredient prices. For every menu item you can see the cost per serving, the food cost percentage, and the gross profit margin — giving you the data you need to price correctly and identify where you're losing margin.

| Metric | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Cost per serving** | Total ingredient cost to produce one portion |
| **Food cost %** | Ingredient cost as a percentage of the selling price |
| **Gross margin** | Revenue minus ingredient cost, per item |

Update an ingredient price and every recipe and margin figure recalculates automatically.

## Waste & Restocking

Every inventory movement is recorded and timestamped, creating a complete audit trail with no gaps. Whether it's a stock delivery from a supplier, a waste event recorded by a chef, an automatic recipe deduction from a sale, a manual adjustment, or a correction — every entry shows the user who made it, the reason, and the exact time.

**Movement types tracked:**

- **Stock Added** — new deliveries and restocks from suppliers
- **Restocks** — supplier-tagged incoming stock
- **Waste** — waste events logged with reason (spoilage, breakage, over-prep)
- **Auto Deductions** — recipe-based deductions from every sale
- **Adjustments** — manual corrections with required reason
- **Corrections** — manager-level amendments to discrepancies

This audit trail makes investigations, supplier disputes, and cost reviews straightforward and accountable.

## Modifier Management

Build modifier groups — such as Extra Cheese, Thin Crust, No Onions, or Add Bacon — and assign them to entire menu categories or individual products. Modifiers support price adjustments, so add-ons that carry a cost are reflected in the order total automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a modifier group">
    Name the group (e.g. "Pizza Extras") and add the individual modifier options with their prices.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign to a category or product">
    Link the modifier group to a category (applies to all items in it) or to individual products.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Modifiers appear at point of sale">
    Staff see the modifier options when adding the item to an order on POS or Waiter App.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Modifier groups are reusable — create them once and assign them to as many categories or products as needed.

## Auto-Unavailability

When a menu item's linked recipe ingredients run critically low, DishInk automatically blocks that item from being ordered — preventing your team from selling dishes you can't make. You control the threshold with the **Min Servings Before Block** setting (configurable from 1 to 100 servings).

When an item is blocked, you choose how it appears to customers and staff:

- **Hidden** — the item disappears from the menu entirely
- **Greyed out** — the item remains visible but cannot be selected

A low-stock badge showing estimated remaining servings appears before the block threshold is reached, giving your team early warning.

## Inventory Overrides

Sometimes stock counts are wrong — a delivery wasn't logged, or waste wasn't recorded. Authorised managers can temporarily override a blocked item to mark it as available so service continues while the records are corrected.

Every override is fully logged with the manager's name, timestamp, and reason. Overrides expire automatically based on your configured rule:

- **End of day** — override clears at midnight
- **On restock** — override clears when a stock addition is recorded
- **After fixed hours** — override clears after a set number of hours

<Note>
  **Inventory permissions** — the following permission flags control access to inventory features:
  - `can_view_inventory` — view stock levels and movement history
  - `can_manage_inventory` — add stock, log waste, and make adjustments
  - `can_manage_recipes` — create and edit recipes
  - `can_override_inventory` — temporarily override availability blocks (manager-level)
  - `can_view_food_cost` — access food cost and margin data
</Note>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does inventory update automatically on every sale?">
    Yes. Every sale through POS or Waiter App triggers recipe-based deductions in real time. Each ingredient linked to the sold item is deducted by its exact recipe quantity — automatically, with no manual step required.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I track waste?">
    Open any inventory item and log a waste event. You enter the quantity wasted and a reason (e.g. spoilage, breakage, over-prep). The waste event is timestamped and logged against your user account, creating a permanent audit record. You can filter waste events in the movement history.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does Auto-Unavailability work?">
    Auto-Unavailability blocks a menu item from being ordered when the estimated remaining servings fall below your **Min Servings Before Block** threshold (1–100). DishInk calculates remaining servings from current ingredient stock levels and the linked recipe quantities. Blocked items can be hidden from the menu or shown greyed out, depending on your setting.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I track multiple suppliers?">
    Yes. You can add multiple suppliers to your inventory and tag each ingredient or stock addition to the relevant supplier. This makes it easy to log deliveries by supplier, view purchase history per supplier, and compare costs across multiple sources for the same ingredient.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does food cost percentage work?">
    Food cost percentage is calculated automatically as: *(total ingredient cost ÷ menu item selling price) × 100*. DishInk pulls the ingredient costs from your inventory prices and the quantities from your linked recipe. When you update an ingredient price, the food cost percentage for every recipe using that ingredient recalculates immediately.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can managers override inventory blocks?">
    Yes. Authorised managers can temporarily mark a blocked item as available. The override is logged with the manager's name and timestamp. You configure when overrides expire: at end of day, when a restock is recorded, or after a fixed number of hours.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
