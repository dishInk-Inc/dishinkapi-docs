Internet outages always happen at the worst possible time — a full house on a Friday night, a busy lunch rush, a special event. Most POS systems stop dead when the connection drops. DishInk is built differently. Three automatic layers of protection keep your POS, Kitchen Display, Waiter App, and Table Management running through any connectivity failure — with zero data loss and zero action required from your team.

## Three-Layer Architecture

DishInk detects which connectivity layer is available and uses the best one automatically. Your team sees no change in their screens or workflow — the system handles the switch silently in the background.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Layer 1 — Online (Cloud Sync)">
    **Normal operating mode.** All devices sync in real time to the DishInk cloud. Every order, payment, status update, and stock movement is instantly available across all devices and locations.

    - Full real-time sync across all devices and locations
    - Live analytics, inventory, and payroll data
    - Every transaction recorded to cloud immediately
  </Step>
  <Step title="Layer 2 — LAN Mode (WiFi Only, No Internet)">
    **Internet is down but your WiFi router is still on.** Devices talk to each other directly over your local network. Your POS still fires orders to the KDS. The Waiter App still sends carts. Table statuses still update. Payments are recorded locally. Service continues without interruption.

    - Orders fire from POS and Waiter App to KDS over WiFi
    - Table statuses stay in sync across devices on the network
    - Payments recorded locally and queued for cloud sync
    - Requires WiFi router to be powered on and functioning
  </Step>
  <Step title="Layer 3 — Fully Offline (No WiFi, No Internet)">
    **Complete connectivity failure.** Each device works fully independently, saving every action to its own local storage. Nothing waits for a connection. When any form of connectivity returns, all queued actions sync to the cloud automatically.

    - Every action saved to device local storage the instant it happens
    - Each device operates independently — no dependency on other devices
    - Queue is persistent through app restarts, device sleep, and browser refreshes
    - Auto-syncs to cloud the moment WiFi or internet is restored
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Local Storage & Auto-Sync

DishInk uses a **local-first** approach: every action is saved to the device's local storage the instant it happens, before the system even attempts to reach the cloud. The cloud sync is always secondary — your data is never at risk from a connection drop.

**How the sync queue works:**

1. An order, payment, or status update is saved locally — instantaneous, no connection required
2. If online, the action syncs to the cloud immediately
3. If offline, the action joins a persistent sync queue on the device
4. When connectivity returns (WiFi or internet), the queue syncs automatically — no manual step

The sync queue is **persistent**. It survives closing the browser tab, restarting the app, putting the device to sleep, and device reboots. Nothing in the queue is ever lost.

<Note>
  **Zero setup required.** Offline protection is always active from the moment you open DishInk on any device. There are no settings to enable, no switches to flip, and no IT configuration needed. It just works.
</Note>

## LAN Monitor

The LAN Monitor is a manager-only page that gives you a real-time view of your network status in plain language — no technical knowledge required.

**What the LAN Monitor shows:**

- Live connection status — Online, LAN Only, or Offline
- Which devices are currently on the network, listed by name
- Queue count — how many actions are waiting to sync per device
- Last synced timestamp per device
- Flagged items that need attention

**Force sync** — if you want to manually trigger a sync rather than waiting for the automatic cycle, tap Force Sync on any device from the LAN Monitor.

<Warning>
  The LAN Monitor is a manager-level page. Staff members cannot access it unless explicitly granted the `can_access_lan` permission. This keeps your network visibility clean and prevents accidental changes.
</Warning>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need WiFi for LAN mode?">
    Yes. LAN mode requires your WiFi router to be powered on and functioning. You do not need an active internet connection — LAN mode works with WiFi only. As long as your router is on and your devices are connected to the same WiFi network, all devices can communicate with each other directly over your local network.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if a device goes fully offline?">
    The device switches automatically to fully offline mode and saves every action — orders, payments, status updates — to its local storage. The queue is persistent through restarts and sleep. When any form of connectivity returns, all queued actions sync to the cloud automatically with no manual step from your team.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I take payments offline?">
    Yes. Cash payments work exactly as normal — recorded immediately to local storage. Card and mobile money payments are also recorded immediately and queued for cloud sync. From the guest's perspective, nothing changes. When connectivity returns, all payment records sync automatically.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a risk of data loss?">
    No. DishInk saves every action locally first, before attempting the cloud sync. Your data is never dependent on an active connection to be recorded. The local queue is persistent through device restarts and browser refreshes. When connectivity is restored, the cloud receives everything — nothing is skipped or lost.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How many devices does offline support cover?">
    Up to 10 devices per location. This covers all typical setups: multiple waiter phones, one or more KDS screens, POS terminals at the bar and front desk, and a manager tablet — all staying in sync over LAN and all falling back to fully offline mode independently when needed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if the router loses power?">
    Each device automatically falls back to fully offline mode and operates completely independently. Devices can no longer communicate with each other, but every device continues saving all actions to its own local storage. When the router comes back online, all devices reconnect and sync their queued actions automatically.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
