This page covers the most common issues operators run into with DishInk and walks you through the steps to resolve them quickly. Topics include connectivity and offline sync problems, POS printing and payment issues, KDS display failures, staff login errors, menu and inventory discrepancies, and QR menu or website issues. If a fix here does not resolve your problem, contact the support team directly on the [Contact](/help/contact) page.

## Connectivity & Offline Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Orders are not reaching the kitchen when the internet is down">
    When the internet drops, orders should automatically continue flowing to the KDS over your local network via LAN mode. If they are not, work through these steps:

    1. **Confirm LAN mode is active.** Open the manager dashboard and navigate to the LAN Monitor page. Check that LAN mode shows as enabled.
    2. **Verify all devices are on the same WiFi network.** Your POS, KDS, and Waiter App must all be connected to the same WiFi router. If any device has dropped off the network or switched to mobile data, LAN sync will not work.
    3. **Check the LAN Monitor queue.** The LAN Monitor page shows pending and synced orders. If orders are queued there, LAN mode is active and orders will fire once device connectivity is confirmed.
    4. **Restart the affected device's WiFi.** Toggle WiFi off and back on, then re-open the DishInk app and check again.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Data is not syncing after the internet reconnects">
    DishInk syncs automatically when your connection is restored, but if you are not seeing your data update, try these steps:

    1. **Pull down to refresh** on any device running DishInk — this triggers a manual sync check.
    2. **Open the LAN Monitor page** in the manager dashboard and review the queue count. A non-zero queue means items are still pending sync; give it 1–2 minutes.
    3. **Force sync from the LAN Monitor page.** Use the manual force sync option if the queue is not clearing automatically.
    4. **Check your internet connection** is stable. Intermittent connectivity can delay sync completion. Once the connection is steady, sync will complete automatically.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A device is showing as 'offline' unexpectedly">
    If a device shows offline when you expect it to be connected, follow these steps:

    1. **Check the device's WiFi connection.** Open the device settings and confirm it is connected to the correct network — not a guest network or mobile hotspot.
    2. **Restart the DishInk app** on the affected device. Close it fully and reopen it; DishInk will attempt to reconnect automatically on launch.
    3. **Restart the device's WiFi.** Toggle WiFi off and on, then wait a few seconds for it to reconnect before reopening the app.
    4. **Check your router.** If multiple devices are showing offline simultaneously, the issue may be with the router itself. Restart the router and allow 1–2 minutes for devices to reconnect.

    DishInk auto-reconnects as soon as the network becomes available — no manual steps are required once WiFi is restored.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## POS Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The POS is not printing receipts">
    If receipts are not printing, work through these steps in order:

    1. **Check the printer connection.** For Bluetooth printers, open the device's Bluetooth settings and confirm the printer is paired and connected. For network printers, confirm the printer is on the same WiFi network as the POS device.
    2. **Confirm the printer is powered on** and has paper loaded. Check for any error lights or flashing indicators on the printer itself.
    3. **Run a test print from POS settings.** Navigate to Settings → Printer in the DishInk POS and use the test print function to confirm the connection between the app and the printer.
    4. **Verify ESC/POS compatibility.** DishInk supports standard ESC/POS thermal printers. If your printer is not ESC/POS compatible, it will not work with DishInk. Contact [dishink26@gmail.com](mailto:dishink26@gmail.com) to confirm compatibility before purchasing a printer.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="An order placed on the POS is not appearing on the KDS">
    If orders are being placed on the POS but not showing on the kitchen display, check the following:

    1. **Confirm the KDS device is on the same network** as the POS. Both must be on the same WiFi router — not different networks or one on mobile data.
    2. **Check that the KDS screen is open and active.** If the KDS device has gone to sleep or the app is running in the background, wake it up and bring the KDS app to the foreground.
    3. **Verify the KDS station is assigned to the menu item.** In your menu settings, check that the items being ordered have a KDS station assigned to them. Items without a station assignment will not route to any KDS screen.
    4. **Check the LAN Monitor** in the manager dashboard for any connectivity issues between the POS and KDS.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A payment is not being recorded in the system">
    If a payment was taken but is not appearing in transaction history, try the following:

    1. **Confirm the payment method is enabled.** Go to Settings → Payment Methods and verify that the method used (cash, MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, card, etc.) is toggled on.
    2. **Check for an active connection.** Payment recording requires either an internet connection or an active LAN connection. If both were unavailable, the payment will be stored locally and will sync once connectivity is restored.
    3. **Retry the payment** on the same order and check your transaction history immediately afterwards. If the original attempt is there alongside the retry, void the duplicate.
    4. **Review transaction history** in the POS or manager dashboard. Locally stored payments sync automatically — check again after a few minutes if you were offline during the transaction.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Staff & Login Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="A staff member can't log in with their PIN">
    If a staff member's PIN is not working, try the following:

    1. **Verify the PIN digits.** DishInk PINs are 4–6 digits. Confirm the staff member is entering the correct PIN and not accidentally including extra digits.
    2. **Manager reset the PIN.** A manager or owner can reset any staff member's PIN from the **Staff Management** section in the dashboard. Navigate to Staff Management → select the staff member → reset PIN.
    3. **Check that the account is active.** In Staff Management, confirm the staff member's account status is set to active and has not been deactivated. Deactivated accounts cannot log in until reactivated.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A manager can't access certain features or modules">
    If a manager account cannot see or use certain features, the issue is likely a permissions setting:

    1. **Check the role template.** Navigate to **Staff Management → Role Templates** and review the permissions assigned to the manager's role. Some features may be restricted to owner-level accounts only.
    2. **Update the role template.** The account owner can edit role templates to add or remove permissions. Changes apply to all staff assigned that role immediately.
    3. **Confirm the correct account type** is being used. Some features in DishInk are only accessible from the primary owner account and cannot be delegated through role templates.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A manager has forgotten their password">
    Use the **"Forgot password?"** link on the DishInk login page at [dishink.com/auth](https://dishink.com/auth). Enter the email address linked to your manager account and follow the password reset instructions sent to that inbox. If you do not receive the reset email within a few minutes, check your spam folder or contact support at [dishink26@gmail.com](mailto:dishink26@gmail.com).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Menu & Inventory Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="A menu item is not appearing to customers">
    If a menu item is not showing on your digital menu or website, check these settings:

    1. **Confirm the item is set to "Available."** Open the item in your dashboard and check that its status is not marked as sold out or hidden. Toggle availability back to available and save.
    2. **Check the item's category is active.** If the category the item belongs to is hidden or deactivated, all items within it will also be hidden. Activate the category and the items inside it will reappear.
    3. **Verify changes were saved.** After making any edits, confirm you saved the changes. Published changes go live instantly — if the item is still not appearing after a few seconds, try a hard refresh on the menu page.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Inventory is not deducting after sales">
    If your stock levels are not decreasing when items are sold, the most likely cause is a missing recipe link:

    1. **Check that the menu item has a recipe linked.** Navigate to Inventory → Recipes and confirm the item has an associated recipe. Items without a linked recipe do not trigger automatic stock deductions.
    2. **Verify the ingredients in the recipe exist in your inventory.** If an ingredient in the recipe has not been added to your inventory list, deductions for that ingredient will not occur.
    3. **Confirm ingredients have stock recorded.** An ingredient must have an initial stock quantity logged before deductions can be tracked. Navigate to Inventory → Ingredients and check the stock quantity for each ingredient in the recipe.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="An item is showing as unavailable even though you have stock">
    This is usually caused by a minimum servings threshold or an unlogged delivery:

    1. **Check the Min Servings Before Block threshold.** In Inventory Settings, each ingredient can have a minimum threshold set — when stock falls below this level, linked menu items are automatically blocked. Review the threshold for the relevant ingredient and adjust it if needed.
    2. **Log any unrecorded deliveries.** If stock arrived but was not logged as a stock-in entry, the system will not know you have it. Add the delivery in Inventory → Stock In to update your available quantity.
    3. **Use an Inventory Override.** If you need to make the item available immediately while you resolve the root cause, a manager can apply an Inventory Override to temporarily unblock the item.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## QR Menu & Website Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The QR code is not working when scanned">
    If your QR code is not scanning or is leading to an error, try these steps:

    1. **Confirm you are using the QR code downloaded from your DishInk dashboard.** Third-party QR generators linked to your DishInk URL will not behave the same as DishInk's dynamic QR codes.
    2. **Test the scan with different phones.** Try scanning with both Android and iOS devices to isolate whether the issue is device-specific.
    3. **Check that your DishInk account is active.** If your subscription has lapsed or your trial has ended without selecting a plan, your menu page will go offline and the QR code will not resolve.
    4. **Clean or replace the printed code.** If the printed QR code is damaged, dirty, or heavily creased, it may not scan reliably. Download a fresh copy from your dashboard and reprint it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The website or digital menu is not showing the latest changes">
    DishInk menu and website changes go live instantly when you save them. If you are not seeing updates:

    1. **Do a hard refresh** on the menu page: press **Ctrl + F5** on Windows/Linux or **Cmd + Shift + R** on Mac.
    2. **Clear your browser cache.** Cached versions of the page can show outdated content. Clear the cache in your browser settings and reload.
    3. **Check on a different device.** If the changes appear on a second device but not the first, the issue is a cached page on the original device. Clear the cache there and reload.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The booking button is not appearing on the website">
    The booking button only appears when a booking URL has been saved in your settings:

    1. **Go to Settings → Bookings** in your DishInk dashboard.
    2. **Paste your booking widget URL** from your third-party booking platform (e.g., Dineplan, OpenTable, SevenRooms, or Resy).
    3. **Save the settings.** The "Book Now" button will appear on your website and digital menu immediately after saving. If it does not appear within a few seconds, do a hard refresh on the page.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
