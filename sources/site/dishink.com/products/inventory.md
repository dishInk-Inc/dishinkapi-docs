Dishink Inventory FAQ

## Got questions? We've got answers.

Quick answers about DishInk Inventory & Food Cost.

01

Does inventory update automatically when products are sold?

Yes. Inventory is automatically deducted based on linked recipes whenever a sale is made from POS, Waiter App.

02

Can I create recipes?

Yes. Recipes can be built with specific ingredients and quantities, then linked directly to menu items for automatic deductions.

03

Can I track waste?

Yes. Waste, restocks, and manual adjustments are all recorded in inventory history with full accountability.

04

Can I calculate food costs?

Yes. DishInk automatically calculates food costs, margins, and cost percentages based on recipe ingredients.

05

What happens to a menu item when stock is low or done ?

A low-stock badge with the estimated number of servings left. Once the item can no longer be made, it is automatically marked as unavailable. Depending on your settings, unavailable items can either be hidden from the menu or shown greyed out until the item is restocked.

06

Can modifiers be linked to menu categories?

Yes. Modifier groups can be assigned to categories and individual products with price adjustments.

07

What does Auto-Unavailability do?

When enabled, DishInk automatically blocks menu items from being ordered when their linked recipe ingredients run critically low. This prevents customers from ordering items you can't make.

08

Can I hide out-of-stock items instead of greying them out?

Yes. You can choose to either hide unavailable items completely from the menu or display them greyed out so customers can still see them but can't order. This is controlled in Inventory Settings.

09

What is the Min Servings Before Block setting?

This sets the threshold for auto-unavailability. For example, if set to 3, a menu item will be blocked when fewer than 3 servings can be made from remaining stock. You can set this between 1 and 100.

10

How do staff overrides work?

Managers can temporarily mark a blocked item as available useful when a delivery wasn't logged or stock was miscounted. Overrides can expire at end of day, when a restock is recorded, or after a fixed number of hours. Every override is logged with the user and timestamp.
