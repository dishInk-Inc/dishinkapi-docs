The Problem

## Challenges restaurant teams face every day.

Running a full-service restaurant means juggling dozens of moving parts. When systems don't talk to each other, things fall through the cracks.

Paper-based orders

Orders written on notepads get lost, misread, or arrive late to the kitchen, especially during a busy service when every minute counts.

Kitchen miscommunication

Waiters and kitchen staff often work off different information, leading to wrong orders, forgotten items, and frustrated customers.

Slow billing

Generating bills, applying discounts, and splitting checks between guests takes too long, creating bottlenecks right when tables need to turn over.

Poor inventory visibility

Stock levels are unknown until an ingredient runs out mid-service, and without recipe tracking, your real food cost is just a guess.

Manual payroll and scheduling

Building shift schedules and calculating wages, tips, and deductions by hand every month is time-consuming, repetitive, and easy to get wrong.

Flying blind on performance

Without kitchen, menu, staff, and sales analytics in one place, you end up making decisions about your business based on gut feeling instead of data.

A Typical Service Flow

## From seated to settled, fully connected.

Here's how a typical service flows through DishInk. Every step syncs automatically across the platform.

Customer is seated

Scans Qr code and Table status is updated to Occupied

Waiter come & takes order

Using the Waiter App on their device

Kitchen receives order

Ticket appears instantly on KDS

Kitchen prepares and updates

Status moves through preparing to ready

Customer tracks progress

Via table QR code on their phone

Bill generated and paid

Through POS with split billing support

Inventory updates

Recipe ingredients deducted automatically

Analytics update

Sales, costs, and performance in real time
