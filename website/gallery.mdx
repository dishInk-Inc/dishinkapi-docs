The gallery is the photo section on your public website. Images you add here appear in the Gallery section of your home page. Go to **Front Operations → Website Gallery** to manage it.

## Uploading images

Click the upload area and select one or more images from your device. Supported formats are PNG and JPG up to 5 MB each.

Multiple images upload in parallel. Each image goes through two stages before appearing in the gallery:

- **Preparing** — the image is compressed to optimise load speed on your website
- **Uploading** — the compressed image is transferred to storage

A progress bar appears on each image tile while it processes. Once done, the image moves from the uploading section into the main gallery grid automatically.

<Note>
  Uploads continue in the background. You do not need to stay on this page — navigate away and come back; completed images will be in the gallery.
</Note>

### If an upload fails

A red error overlay appears on the failed tile with two buttons:

- **Retry** (↺) — attempts the upload again
- **Remove** (×) — discards the failed image

Check your internet connection before retrying. Multiple failures usually indicate a connectivity problem.

## Plan limits

| Plan | Image limit |
| --- | --- |
| Starter | 5 images |
| Growth | 20 images |
| Growth Pro | Unlimited |
| Enterprise | Unlimited |

The header shows your current count against your limit (e.g. "7 / 20 images used"). When you reach the limit, the upload area is hidden and an upgrade banner appears.

## Deleting an image

Hover over any image in the gallery grid and click the red trash button that appears in the top-right corner. Confirm the prompt to delete permanently.

<Warning>
  Deleted images are removed from both the gallery and your storage permanently. This cannot be undone — reupload the image if you need it back.
</Warning>

## Permissions

Staff with `can_view_gallery` can see the gallery page. Staff with `can_manage_gallery` can upload and delete images. Without `can_manage_gallery`, the upload area is hidden and delete buttons don't appear — the page is read-only.

## Tips & best practices

<Tip>
  Upload landscape photos (wider than tall) for the best appearance in the gallery grid. Portrait photos work but may crop awkwardly depending on your website theme.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Use real photos of your food and space — not stock images. Customers convert better when they can see the actual restaurant before visiting.
</Tip>

## Common mistakes

<Warning>
  Do not close the browser tab while images are uploading. The upload will be interrupted. Reopen the gallery page and retry the failed images.
</Warning>
