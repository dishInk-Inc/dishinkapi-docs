DishInk turns WhatsApp into a full ordering channel — customers browse your menu, build a cart, and place orders straight into your dashboard, all inside a familiar WhatsApp chat.

## Overview

- Guests message your restaurant's WhatsApp number.
- DishInk sends a menu link tied to your live products, prices, and availability.
- Guests build a cart, choose delivery or pickup, and confirm.
- The order lands in the [unified order queue](/whatsapp/order-management) with a KDS ticket.
- Order status updates (accepted, preparing, ready) are pushed back to WhatsApp automatically.

## Why WhatsApp

Across African markets, WhatsApp is the dominant messaging platform. Customers order in a channel they already trust — no app to install, no login, no learning curve.

## What you need

| Requirement | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| WhatsApp Business number | Or WhatsApp Business API for scale |
| DishInk menu | Configured with photos and prices |
| Delivery zones or pickup config | Under Settings → Ordering |
| Payment options | Cash, mobile money, card link |

<Note>
  WhatsApp ordering shares the same menu, inventory, and reports as the POS. There is no separate menu to maintain.
</Note>

## Tips & best practices

<Tip>
  Publish the WhatsApp link on every table QR code and every social post. Discovery is 80% of WhatsApp order volume.
</Tip>

## Common mistakes

<Warning>
  Do not use a personal WhatsApp for orders. Personal accounts cannot access the Business API and shifts cannot share the number.
</Warning>
