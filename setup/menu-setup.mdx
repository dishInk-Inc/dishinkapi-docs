Your DishInk menu is a single source of truth shared across every customer touchpoint and operational tool — your QR codes, your website, the POS, the Waiter App, and WhatsApp ordering all pull from the same menu you build here. Update a price or mark an item as sold out once and the change goes live everywhere instantly, with no reprinting ever required. This guide covers building your menu from scratch, using AI to import an existing one, and managing items day to day.

## Build Your Menu from Scratch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Menu section">
    In your DishInk dashboard, go to **Menu** in the left-hand navigation. This is where you create and manage all categories and items.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your categories">
    Select **Add Category** and name each section of your menu. Common categories include:
    - Starters
    - Mains
    - Grills
    - Drinks
    - Desserts

    Drag categories to reorder them. The order you set here is the order customers see when they scan your QR code or visit your menu online.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add items to each category">
    Inside each category, select **Add Item** and fill in:
    - **Name** — the dish or drink name as it appears to customers
    - **Description** — a short, appetising description
    - **Price** — displayed in your local currency
    - **Photo** — upload directly from your phone or desktop
    - **Allergen notes** — flag common allergens (gluten, dairy, nuts, etc.) so guests can make informed choices

    Repeat for each item in the category.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set item availability">
    Use the **Available / Sold Out** toggle on any item to control whether it shows as orderable. Toggling an item to sold out updates your live menu immediately — customers see the change the next time they scan or refresh. Toggle it back to available when you restock.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add modifiers">
    Modifiers let customers customise items — for example, Extra Cheese, Thin Crust, or Spicy. To set them up:

    1. Go to **Menu → Modifiers → Create Modifier Group** (e.g., "Crust Type", "Extras")
    2. Add options to the group and set a price adjustment for each (leave at K0 for free options)
    3. Assign the modifier group to a category or individual item

    Modifiers appear to customers when they add an item to their order and fire through to the KDS with the order so the kitchen sees exactly what to prepare.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link recipes for inventory deduction">
    If you are on the Operations Suite or Enterprise plan and using the Inventory module, link each menu item to its recipe. Go to **Menu → [Item] → Recipe** and add each ingredient with the exact quantity used per serving. When that item sells through the POS or Waiter App, DishInk automatically deducts the right amounts from your stock in real time — no manual counting required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## AI Menu Extraction

<Info>
  AI Menu Extraction is available on **Growth, Operations Suite, and Enterprise** plans (2 uses per month, resetting on your billing date). Activation requires the one-time onboarding fee.
</Info>

If you already have a printed or digital menu, you can skip manual data entry entirely.

1. Go to **Menu → AI Menu Extraction**
2. Upload a PDF or a photo of your existing menu — even a handwritten one
3. The AI reads your menu, structures the categories, and extracts all item names, descriptions, and prices
4. Review every item before confirming — nothing goes live without your approval
5. Make any edits, then publish

The AI performs best with clear, well-lit photos or digital PDFs. After extraction, your menu is fully editable like any other DishInk menu.

## Live Updates

<Info>
  Every change you save in your dashboard goes live instantly on your website and QR menu. There is no publish button to click and no delay. Because DishInk QR codes are dynamic, the code itself never changes — it always points to the same permanent link. You never need to reprint QR codes when you update your menu.
</Info>

## Menu-Only Mode

If you already have a website and want to use DishInk purely as a digital menu — without building a full DishInk website — enable **Menu-Only Mode** under **Settings → Website**.

In Menu-Only Mode:
- Your DishInk link goes directly to your menu, skipping the homepage
- You can share this link via QR code, WhatsApp, or a button on your existing website
- You can add an external link (e.g., "Back to our website") in your DishInk navigation so customers can return to your main site

Menu-Only Mode works on all plans and requires no extra configuration beyond toggling the setting.

## Photo Tips

<Tip>
  DishInk automatically optimises every photo you upload for fast mobile loading — large files are compressed without visible quality loss. For the best results:

  - Use **square or landscape** ratio images (1:1 or 4:3)
  - Shoot in good natural light against a clean background
  - On **Growth, Operations Suite, and Enterprise** plans, use **AI Image Import** to search and import high-quality, royalty-free food photos from Pexels and Pixabay directly inside your dashboard — no need to source images externally (2 uses per month; requires the one-time onboarding fee to activate)
</Tip>
