## Overview

Staff can request an advance on their upcoming pay slip directly from the **Staff Earnings** page. Managers approve or reject requests from the **Advances** tab on the Admin Payroll page. Approved advances are automatically deducted from the next generated payroll record.

Advances protect both sides: staff get emergency cash without a loan; the business caps exposure with per-request and monthly limits.

<Frame>
  ![Salary Advances panel with Pending, Staff Overview, and Limits tabs](/images/payroll/salary-advances.png)
</Frame>

## The three tabs

### Pending

All advance requests awaiting decision. Each row shows staff name, requested amount, reason, request date, and remaining monthly cap.

**Actions:** Approve, Reject.

### Staff Overview

Every staff member with their advance activity: total requested, total approved, outstanding balance, month-to-date used.

### Limits

Global defaults and per-staff overrides.

| Limit | Default | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Per request** | K1,000 | Maximum a staff member can request in a single request |
| **Monthly cap** | K2,000 | Maximum a staff member can be advanced in a calendar month |

<Note>
  A per-request limit **cannot exceed** the monthly cap. If you raise the per-request limit above the monthly cap, the monthly cap wins.
</Note>

## Request flow (staff)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Staff Earnings">
    From the staff dashboard, tap **Advance**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check available amount">
    Shows remaining per-request limit and monthly cap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter amount and reason">
    Reason is required so managers have context (medical, transport, family, etc.).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Request is created with status **pending**. Staff can see it in their advance history.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Approval flow (manager)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Advances tab">
    Go to **Payroll → Advances**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review pending requests">
    Check the staff member's month-to-date usage, reason, and remaining cap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve or Reject">
    Add a note on rejection. On approval, funds are marked disbursed — arrange the actual cash / mobile-money transfer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deduction happens automatically">
    Next time payroll is generated for that staff member, the advance is deducted from gross pay and its status flips to **deducted**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Setting limits

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Limits tab">
    From the Advances panel, click **Limits**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set global defaults">
    Adjust per-request and monthly cap for all staff.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Override per staff">
    On the Staff Overview tab, click a staff row and set custom limits. Useful for senior staff or trusted long-term employees.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips

- **Approve within 24 hours** — fast decisions build trust; slow ones cause staff to seek external loans.
- Use **staff overview** to spot patterns — a staff member hitting the monthly cap every month may need a wage review.
- Set **higher limits for managers** who have more stable schedules.
- Always **record disbursement method** in the manager note (bank, mobile money, cash) for the audit trail.

## Common mistakes

- **Approving above the monthly cap.** DishInk blocks this by default — raise the cap first if you need to approve more.
- **Forgetting to disburse** after approval. Approval marks it disbursed for accounting, but you still need to move the money.
- **Deducting manually on top of auto-deduction.** Approved advances deduct automatically — don't add a second deduction line, or the staff will be double-charged.
- **Rejecting without a reason.** Staff feel dismissed. Always write a one-line reason.
