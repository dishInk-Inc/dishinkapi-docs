## Overview

**Payroll Settings** is your one-stop control panel for how DishInk calculates every pay slip. These settings apply to your entire venue and take effect on the **next** generated payroll run — approved records are never retroactively changed.

  ![Payroll Settings panel with OT rules and proration options](/images/payroll/settings.png)
</Frame>

## Settings

### Overtime threshold hours

The number of hours per shift before overtime kicks in.

- **Default:** 8
- **Range:** 4–12
- Applies to all hourly wage structures unless overridden per staff.

### Overtime multiplier

How much overtime is paid relative to the base rate.

- **1.5×** (time-and-a-half) — most common
- **2×** (double time) — Sundays, public holidays

<Note>
  If a specific wage structure has an absolute **OT rate** set (e.g. K20/hr), that overrides the multiplier for that staff member.
</Note>

### Allow negative net pay

Controls what happens when deductions and advances exceed gross pay.

| Value | Behavior |
| --- | --- |
| **On** | Net pay can be negative. Staff owes the difference. Rare — usually disabled. |
| **Off** (default) | Net pay is capped at zero. Remaining deduction / advance balance rolls into the next period. |

### Monthly proration by days

Only applies to **monthly** wage structures.

- **On** — monthly salary is prorated by actual days worked. If a staff member joins mid-month, they get a partial salary.
- **Off** — full monthly salary paid regardless of days present.

### Working days per month

Used for monthly proration.

- **Default:** 26 (standard 6-day work week)
- **Alternatives:** 22 (5-day week), 30 (calendar-day proration)

Formula when proration is on:

```text
daily_equivalent = monthly_salary / working_days_per_month
prorated_pay     = daily_equivalent × days_worked_in_period
```

## Configuring settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Admin Payroll">
    Go to **Payroll → Records** and click the **Settings** button in the toolbar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust each field">
    Change threshold, multiplier, negative-pay toggle, and proration as needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Changes apply from the next payroll generation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips

- Set **OT threshold** to match your standard shift length — 8 hours for most restaurants, 12 for split shifts.
- Keep **allow negative net pay off** unless you have a legal/policy reason otherwise; it protects staff from take-home shocks.
- Use **22 working days** if your operation is Monday–Friday only.
- Review settings at the **start of each financial year** with your accountant.

## Common mistakes

- **Changing settings mid-period** — settings only affect newly generated records, so a mid-period change can create inconsistency between shifts.
- **Working days = 30** but only staffed 26 days a month — proration undercounts pay. Match this to your actual operating schedule.
- **Multiplier 2× as default** — pay ramps up fast on long shifts; only use for premium OT policies.
