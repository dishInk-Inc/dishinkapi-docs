## Overview

Tips are collected during service (card tips at checkout, cash tips at close-out) and distributed to staff by rule. DishInk supports two modes:

- **`equal_split`** — pool all tips for the period and share equally among eligible staff
- **`to_waiter`** — pay tips directly to the waiter who owned the table

Distribution runs in batches. Each batch is recorded so you have a full audit trail of who got what and when.

## The three tabs

### Settings

Configure how tips are distributed.

| Setting | Values | Default |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Mode** | `equal_split`, `to_waiter` | `equal_split` |
| **Pay after** | `shift`, `week`, `month` | `week` |
| **Active staff roles** | Multi-select: waiter, cashier, kitchen, bar, manager | waiter, cashier |

<Note>
  **Active staff roles** only applies to `equal_split`. In `to_waiter` mode the waiter who owned the table always receives the tip regardless of role.
</Note>

### Preview

Shows the current unpaid tip pool and how it will distribute given current settings. Before running a batch, use Preview to spot-check totals and eligible staff.

### History

Every past batch with:

- Batch date
- Period covered
- Total pool amount
- Number of recipients
- Per-recipient breakdown (expand row)

## Modes explained

### Equal split

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pool all tips">
    Every card tip and cash tip captured during the period is added to the pool.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter to eligible staff">
    Only staff whose role is in **active\_staff\_roles** and who clocked in during the period are eligible.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Divide equally">
    Pool total ÷ eligible staff count = per-staff share.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Example:** Pool = K450. Eligible = 3 waiters \+ 2 cashiers = 5 staff. Each gets **K90**.

### To waiter

<Steps>
  <Step title="Attribute each tip to a table">
    Card tips are attributed to the table's assigned waiter at the moment of payment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sum by waiter">
    Each waiter's total = sum of tips on all tables they closed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pay directly">
    Each waiter receives their attributed total. No pooling, no splitting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Cash tips left directly for a specific waiter are also attributed to that waiter in `to_waiter` mode. Cash tips not attributed to a table go to the general pool.
</Note>

## Running a distribution batch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Tips tab">
    Go to **Payroll → Tips**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the Preview tab">
    Verify pool total and eligible staff. Adjust settings if needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Distribute">
    Confirms the batch. Tips move from **unpaid** to **paid** and appear on staff earnings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review in History">
    New batch appears at the top of the History tab with a full breakdown.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Unpaid vs. paid tips

The **Staff Earnings → Tips** tab shows:

- **Unpaid tips** — accumulated but not yet distributed. Value depends on mode.
  - `equal_split`: estimated share of current pool
  - `to_waiter`: actual attributed amount to this staff member
- **Paid tips** — amounts distributed in past batches

## Tips

- Run tip distribution **before generating payroll** so tips appear on the pay slip.
- Match **pay after** to your payroll frequency — weekly payroll \+ weekly tips is simplest.
- Use **`to_waiter`** for full-service restaurants; use **`equal_split`** for casual dining or when back-of-house shares the pool.
- Always **close all shifts** before distributing — open shifts mean tips haven't fully landed.

## Common mistakes

- **Distributing before shifts close.** Unclosed shifts still have pending tips — you'll miss them.
- **Wrong active roles.** If kitchen isn't in `active_staff_roles`, they won't share the pool even if you meant to include them.
- **Running distribution twice for the same period.** The second run distributes only tips added since the first batch — usually K0. If you meant to redo the whole period, void the batch first (contact support for record fixes).
- **Mixing modes mid-period.** Switch modes only at period boundaries — mid-period switches confuse attribution.
