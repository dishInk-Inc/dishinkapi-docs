## Overview

A **wage structure** defines how a staff member is paid: the pay type, the base rate, and (for hourly staff) the overtime rate and threshold. Every staff member on payroll must have an active wage structure — without one, generated pay slips show K0 base pay.

Wage structures are versioned by `effective_from` date. When you raise a wage, set the new effective date; DishInk will use the old rate for periods before that date and the new rate after.

## Wage types

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Hourly" icon="clock">
    Paid per hour clocked. Overtime kicks in after threshold hours per shift.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Daily" icon="calendar-day">
    Fixed daily rate regardless of hours. Best for casual or part-time staff.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Monthly" icon="calendar-days">
    Fixed monthly salary. Optionally prorated by days worked (see [Payroll Settings](/payroll/settings)).
  </Card>

  <Card title="Commission" icon="percent">
    Percentage of attributed sales. Combines with any base rate if set.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Setting a wage

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Admin Payroll">
    Go to **Payroll → Wages**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a staff member">
    Click the staff member's row. If they have no wage yet, click **Set Wage**; otherwise click **Edit**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose type">
    Pick **Hourly**, **Daily**, **Monthly**, or **Commission**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter base rate">
    - Hourly: rate per hour (e.g. K15/hr)
    - Daily: rate per day (e.g. K120/day)
    - Monthly: gross salary (e.g. K5,000/month)
    - Commission: leave zero if pure commission, or set a base retainer
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure overtime (hourly only)">
    - **OT threshold hrs** — hours per shift before OT starts (default: **8**)
    - **OT rate** — either an absolute per-hour rate or a multiplier of base (1.5× or 2×, see [Payroll Settings](/payroll/settings))
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set commission % (commission only)">
    Enter the percentage of attributed sales, e.g. **5%** of drink sales for a bar attendant.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set effective from date">
    Defaults to today. For a scheduled raise, pick a future date; the current wage stays active until then.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    The new wage becomes the active structure for that staff member from the effective date forward.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Overtime calculation

For hourly staff, overtime is calculated **per shift**, not per period:

```text
Regular hours = min(shift_hours, OT_threshold)
OT hours      = max(0, shift_hours - OT_threshold)
Base pay      = Regular hours × base_rate
OT pay        = OT hours × (base_rate × OT_multiplier)  OR  OT hours × OT_rate
```

**Example:** Base rate K15/hr, OT threshold 8, OT multiplier 1.5×. Staff works a 10-hour shift.

- Regular: 8 × K15 = **K120**
- Overtime: 2 × (K15 × 1.5) = **K45**
- Shift total: **K165**

## Wage history

Every wage change is recorded. The **Wages** tab shows the current active wage; click **History** on a staff row to see every past wage with its effective range.

<Note>
  Retroactive wage changes only affect payroll records **generated after** the change. Approved records use the wage that was active when they were generated.
</Note>

## Tips

- Keep wage structures **consistent within roles** — same rate for all cashiers, same for all waiters.
- Use **effective from** for scheduled raises so payroll runs correctly around the raise date.
- For **commission staff**, always attribute sales at close-out — see [Staff Accounts](/staff/staff-accounts).
- Review wages **annually** for compliance with Zambian minimum wage regulations.

## Common mistakes

- **No active wage.** New hires generate K0 pay slips until a wage is saved.
- **OT threshold too low.** If your standard shift is 9 hours, an 8-hour threshold means OT every shift — set to match your operation.
- **Commission with no attribution.** Commission staff must be linked to sales at checkout, or their pay will be zero.
- **Editing an approved wage retroactively.** Won't change past pay slips — you need to adjust the record manually.
