PIN Login is the fast authentication method for cashiers, waiters, and kitchen staff on shared terminals — a 4–6 digit code that takes under 2 seconds to enter.

## Overview

- Every staff account has a unique PIN.
- PIN login is scoped to the physical terminal.
- Every action taken with a PIN is logged against that staff member.

## Tips & best practices

<Tip>
  Enforce a policy that PINs cannot be sequential (1234) or repeated (0000). DishInk rejects the weakest patterns automatically.
</Tip>

## Common mistakes

<Warning>
  Never share PINs verbally or on paper. Every void, discount, and refund is logged against the PIN — shared PINs erase accountability.
</Warning>
