Products are the sellable items on your menu: burgers, cocktails, pastries, water bottles. Each product is linked to an ingredient recipe so stock is deducted with every sale.

## Overview

- A product is what the customer buys.
- A recipe maps the product to the [ingredients](/inventory/ingredients) it consumes.
- On every sale, DishInk deducts the recipe ingredients from stock automatically.

<Note>
  Products with no recipe are treated as service items — stock is never deducted (e.g. corkage fees).
</Note>

## Tips & best practices

<Tip>
  Build every recipe. Stock alerts and food-cost analytics only work when recipes are complete.
</Tip>

## Common mistakes

<Warning>
  Do not set the same SKU on multiple products. SKUs must be unique per branch or stock movement becomes ambiguous.
</Warning>
