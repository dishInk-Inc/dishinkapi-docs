The Inventory module is where ingredients, recipes, stock movements, and menu availability all meet. When a waiter sends an order, when the kitchen marks it Ready, and when a manager restocks a delivery, Inventory is the system of record that updates the menu across every device — POS, Waiter, KDS, QR menu, and online ordering — in real time.

All amounts are in Zambian Kwacha (**ZMW**).

<Frame>
  ![Inventory dashboard with the seven tabs across the top](/images/inventory/overview-tabs.png)
</Frame>

## Who this is for

| Role | What they do in Inventory |
| --- | --- |
| **Owner / Admin** | Full access. Sets recipes, modifier links, thresholds, override policy, and inventory settings. |
| **Manager** | Restocks, records waste/damage, deactivates staff overrides, reviews the adjustment log. |
| **Cashier / Waiter** | Read-only — sees the _effect_ of Inventory (out-of-stock badges, “~X servings left”). Cannot edit here. |
| **Kitchen Staff** | No direct access — but every completed order auto-deducts ingredients through the recipe. |

## How the pieces fit together

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ingredients are the source of truth">
    Every ingredient has a **unit** (kg, g, l, ml, pieces, portions, boxes, slices, cups), a **category**, and a **current stock** level. Ingredients live in the **Stock** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Recipes turn ingredients into menu items">
    A recipe lists the ingredients (and quantity per serving) needed to make one unit of a menu item. Recipes power both **auto-deduction** and the **food-cost card**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Modifiers extend recipes">
    Optional add-ons (Extra cheese, Add bacon) can link to the same ingredients with a `deduct_quantity`, so add-ons deplete stock correctly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Orders auto-deduct in real time">
    When the kitchen marks an item **Ready**, the recipe’s ingredients are deducted from stock and a row is written to the **Adjustments** log with type `auto_deduction`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Availability updates the menu everywhere">
    If an ingredient drops below the servings threshold, the linked menu items are auto-marked **Unavailable** on POS, Waiter, QR menu, and online ordering — instantly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Managers restock, override, or adjust">
    Deliveries are recorded with **Restock**, spoilage with **Waste** or **Damage**, and blocked items can be temporarily unblocked with an **Override**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key concepts at a glance

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Auto-deduction">
    When a kitchen item is completed, the linked recipe deducts each ingredient by `quantity_per_serving × order_qty`. This runs server-side and is logged as `auto_deduction` in the Adjustments log.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Servings left">
    For any menu item, servings left = `min(ingredient.current_stock / recipe_line.quantity_per_serving)` across every ingredient in the recipe. Ingredients flagged `ignore_stock` are excluded.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Auto-unavailability">
    Controlled in **Inventory Settings**. When enabled, items are auto-blocked as soon as servings-left drops below **Min Servings Before Block**. Waiters see a red _Unavailable_ badge; QR menus can either grey out or hide the item.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Staff overrides">
    A manager can temporarily unblock an auto-unavailable item. Overrides expire on **End of day**, **On restock**, or after a **Fixed duration**. Every override is logged and can be deactivated at any time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Food-cost analysis">
    For each recipe, DishInk sums `quantity_per_serving × ingredient.unit_cost` to get cost-per-serving, then compares it to the menu price: <br />• **Healthy** — food cost \< 30% <br />• **Warning** — 30% – 40% <br />• **Critical** — \> 40%
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tips & best practices

<Tip>
  Set up ingredients **first**, then recipes, then modifiers. Trying to build a recipe before the ingredient exists will slow you down.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Use consistent units. If flour is stocked in **kg**, always express recipe quantities in kg (e.g. `0.25` kg), not grams. Mixing units per ingredient is a common source of food-cost errors.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Turn on **Show Servings Badge** in Inventory Settings during pilot week — it gives waiters an early warning before an item goes unavailable.
</Tip>

## Common mistakes

- **Recipe missing an ingredient** — the item will never auto-deduct or auto-block. Check the recipe’s **Attention** count.
- **Wrong unit on the ingredient** — a cost of `ZMW 45 / kg` becomes `ZMW 45,000 / kg` if you accidentally save it as grams.
- **Ignoring `ignore_stock`** — mark garnishes and negligible items as `ignore_stock` so a missing sprig of parsley doesn’t block the whole dish.
- **Overrides left on** — a manager unblocks an item “just for tonight” but forgets to set an expiry. Use **End of day** or **On restock** as the default.

<Note>
  Inventory settings, recipes, and modifier links are **shared across all branches** of a restaurant. Only stock levels and adjustments are branch-scoped.
</Note>
