An **override** temporarily unblocks a menu item that DishInk has auto-marked **Unavailable** because its ingredient stock ran low. Overrides are the escape hatch: when the kitchen tells you they can still make three more portions but the system doesn’t know, a manager can unblock the item, serve those portions, and let the override expire.

Every override is logged. Nothing is silent.

<Frame>
  ![Overrides tab with Active and History sub-views](/images/inventory/overrides-tab.png)
</Frame>

## Who this is for

- **Manager / Owner** — creates and deactivates overrides. Requires the **Allow Staff Override** setting to be on ([Inventory Settings](/inventory/settings)).
- **Cashier / Waiter** — sees an override-unblocked item as available; the override is transparent to service.

<Note>
  Overrides are stored in the `inventory_overrides` table with the acting manager, timestamp, expiry mode, and any deactivation reason. The full history is retained even after expiry.
</Note>

## When to override

Use an override when **all** of these are true:

1. The item is auto-marked Unavailable.
2. The kitchen confirms they can still serve it (uncounted stock, substitute prep, edge-of-recipe portioning).
3. The unblock is temporary — you plan to restock, adjust the recipe, or let the override expire naturally.

Do **not** override to hide a recipe or ingredient problem. If the same item is being overridden every day, fix the root cause: recalibrate the recipe, adjust `ignore_stock`, or update the ingredient’s minimum stock.

## Create an override

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Overrides tab">
    **Inventory → Overrides**. The **Active** sub-view shows every currently unblocked item.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Add Override">
    Pick the menu item that’s currently auto-marked Unavailable. Only auto-blocked items appear here — you can’t override an item that was manually turned off in Menu Management.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an expiry mode">
    Three options:

    | Mode | When it expires |
    | --- | --- |
    | **End of day** | Automatically at midnight (device time). Best default for a temporary unblock during the current shift. |
    | **On restock** | Automatically when any ingredient in the recipe receives a restock. Best when you’re unblocking because a delivery is imminent. |
    | **Fixed duration** | After N hours (1–24). Best for lunch-service unblocks (“hold this open for 3 hours”). |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a reason (recommended)">
    Free-text — e.g. `Chef has 4 portions plated ready` or `Waiting on afternoon delivery`. Shows in the log and helps the next manager understand the decision.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm with manager PIN">
    The override is created immediately. The menu item is unblocked across POS, Waiter, QR menu, and online ordering within seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Deactivate an override manually

Active overrides can be deactivated at any time before their expiry.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the override">
    **Overrides → Active**, or search by menu item.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Deactivate">
    A confirmation modal asks for a reason (optional) and a manager PIN.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    The menu item is re-evaluated against current stock. If it’s still below the servings threshold, it becomes Unavailable again immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Deactivating an active override in the middle of service will remove the item from the menu on every device. Warn the waiters first.
</Warning>

## Active vs History

- **Active** — currently in force. Each row shows the menu item, expiry mode, remaining time, creating manager, and reason.
- **History** — every past override, including who created it, who deactivated it (or the auto-expiry reason: `end_of_day`, `on_restock`, `timed_expiry`), and the timestamps.

Use History for audits and for spotting patterns — if the same item appears repeatedly, fix the recipe.

## What auto-expiry looks like

| Expiry mode | Trigger | Log entry |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **End of day** | Local midnight | `Auto-expired: end_of_day` |
| **On restock** | Any restock to a linked ingredient (see [Stock Levels](/inventory/stock-levels)) | `Auto-expired: on_restock (restock #A234)` |
| **Fixed duration** | Duration elapsed | `Auto-expired: timed (2h)` |

The menu item re-evaluates against current stock immediately after expiry.

## Tips & best practices

<Tip>
  Default to **End of day**. It’s the safest choice — you never accidentally leave an item overridden across shifts.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Use **On restock** when you know a delivery is coming. It self-cleans the moment the ingredient is back in stock, without a manager having to remember.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Review the History tab weekly. If the same three items are overridden every Friday night, the recipe or the minimum stock level for their key ingredient is wrong.
</Tip>

## Common mistakes

- **Using overrides to hide a broken recipe** — fix the recipe. Overrides are for edge cases, not chronic issues.
- **Fixed duration set too long** — “24 hours” becomes “forgotten override”. Prefer End of day.
- **No reason entered** — the next manager has no context. Always give a one-line justification.
- **Overriding without telling the kitchen** — the item comes back on POS but the kitchen is out of stock. Confirm with the head chef _before_ creating the override.
