A **recipe** is the bridge between a menu item and the ingredients it consumes. Recipes power three of DishInk’s most valuable features:

1. **Auto-deduction** — stock goes down automatically when the kitchen completes an order.
2. **Servings left** — the menu can show “~8 servings left” and auto-block items when they run out.
3. **Food-cost analysis** — every menu item is graded healthy / warning / critical based on cost ÷ price.

<Frame>
  ![Recipe builder with ingredient lines, food-cost card, and attention count](/images/inventory/recipe-builder.png)
</Frame>

## Who this is for

- **Owner / Admin** — builds recipes during onboarding and after menu updates.
- **Head Chef / Manager** — tunes recipe quantities based on actual portioning.

## Build a recipe

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Recipes tab">
    Go to **Inventory → Recipes**. Every menu item is listed with an **Attention** count — the number of ingredient lines that are missing, out-of-stock, or need review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expand the menu item">
    Tap the item card to expand. The **Recipe Builder** appears with the current recipe lines on the left and the **Food Cost Card** on the right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an ingredient line">
    Tap **Add Ingredient**. Search for the ingredient by name. Every ingredient in the Stock tab is available.

    <Note>
      If the ingredient isn’t there, add it in the [Stock tab](/inventory/ingredients) first, then come back.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter quantity per serving">
    How much of this ingredient one serving consumes, in the ingredient’s base unit. Examples:

    - Chicken breast: `0.2` (kg)
    - Salt: `5` (g)
    - Cooking oil: `0.02` (l)
    - Egg: `2` (pieces)

    The Food Cost Card updates live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="(Optional) Flag `ignore_stock`">
    If the ingredient is negligible or always available (salt, pepper, garnish), tick **Ignore stock**. The line still contributes to food cost, but its stock level will never block the menu item.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repeat for every ingredient">
    Add one line per ingredient. There’s no limit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the recipe">
    Tap **Save Recipe**. Changes sync immediately and any pending drafts are cleared.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The recipe editor keeps a **draft** as you type. Unsaved changes are marked with a small indicator on the recipe line and on the item card. Reload-safe: drafts survive a page refresh.
</Note>

## The Food Cost Card

Every recipe shows a Food Cost Card with:

| Metric | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Cost per serving** | `Σ (quantity_per_serving × ingredient.unit_cost)` for every line |
| **Menu price** | The item’s selling price from Menu Management |
| **Food cost %** | `cost_per_serving / menu_price × 100` |
| **Margin** | `menu_price − cost_per_serving` |
| **Projected profit** | Margin × recent sales volume for this item |

Bands:

- **Healthy** — food cost \< 30%
- **Warning** — 30% – 40%
- **Critical** — \> 40%

<Warning>
  A food cost above **40%** is flagged in red on the recipe card and in the Menu Analytics report. Either raise the price, reduce portion size, or negotiate the ingredient cost down.
</Warning>

## Servings-left calculation

DishInk continuously calculates how many servings of a menu item you can still make:

```text
servings_left = min(
  ingredient.current_stock / recipe_line.quantity_per_serving
) across every non-ignored recipe line
```

When servings-left drops below **Min Servings Before Block** (set in [Inventory Settings](/inventory/settings)), the menu item is auto-marked **Unavailable** across POS, Waiter, QR menus, and online ordering.

When servings-left drops below the **Badge Threshold**, a “~X servings left” chip appears on the menu item so waiters and customers know it’s running low.

## Auto-deduction

When the kitchen marks an item **Ready** (or when the waiter serves it, depending on your workflow), the recipe is applied:

```text
for each recipe_line:
  ingredient.current_stock -= quantity_per_serving × order_qty
  write adjustments row (type: auto_deduction)
```

Auto-deductions appear in the [Adjustments log](/inventory/adjustments) with the linked order ID for full traceability.

<Note>
  Voided items and cancelled orders **reverse** the deduction automatically — you’ll see a matching `auto_deduction` reversal row in the log.
</Note>

## Copy a recipe

Menu items that share a base (grilled chicken with different sauces) can share a recipe skeleton. On the Recipe Builder, tap **⋮ → Copy from…** and pick another menu item. The lines are duplicated — tweak from there.

## Tips & best practices

<Tip>
  **Weigh a plate before you build the recipe.** Cook one portion, weigh every ingredient before it hits the pan, and enter those exact numbers. Guessing is the #1 source of food-cost drift.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Ingredients with `ignore_stock` still count toward food cost. Use it liberally for spice-level items so you don’t block dishes over a missing pinch of oregano.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Review the **Attention** count weekly. Any item with attention \> 0 has an issue — usually a deleted ingredient or a recipe line that was never saved.
</Tip>

## Common mistakes

- **Missing recipe** — the item will never auto-deduct and its food cost will show as 0%. The Recipes tab shows these as _No recipe_ in amber.
- **Wrong unit in the quantity** — `2` grams of chicken breast (should be `0.2` kg) will make one delivery last a year on paper.
- **Forgetting garnish / oil** — under-costs the dish. Add every ingredient, even 2 g of salt.
- **Not flagging `ignore_stock`** — a single missing lime blocks 12 menu items.
- **Modifiers not linked** — Extra cheese sells all day but stock never moves. Link the modifier to the ingredient (see [Modifiers](/inventory/modifiers)).
