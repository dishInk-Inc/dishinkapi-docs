Modifiers are the add-ons and choices offered on a menu item — _Extra cheese_, _Add bacon_, _Well done_, _Large size_. Without modifier links, add-ons sell but stock never moves. The **Modifiers** tab in Inventory closes that loop.

<Frame>
  ![Modifier Manager with three tabs: Menu Categories, Modifier Groups, Links](/images/inventory/modifier-manager.png)
</Frame>

## Who this is for

- **Owner / Admin** — designs modifier groups and links them to ingredients.
- **Manager** — adjusts link quantities as portioning changes.

## The three tabs

The Modifier Manager has three side-by-side views:

| Tab | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Menu Categories** | Attach a modifier group to a whole category (e.g. every burger gets _Doneness_). |
| **Modifier Groups** | Create and edit the modifier groups themselves — name, min/max choices, required, options. |
| **Links** | The core of Inventory integration — map each modifier **option** to an ingredient with a `deduct_quantity`. |

## Create a modifier group

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Modifier Groups">
    **Inventory → Modifiers → Modifier Groups**, then **New Group**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the group">
    Examples: _Doneness_, _Add-ons_, _Size_, _Sauce_.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the rules">
    - **Required** — the customer must choose an option (e.g. _Doneness_ on a steak).
    - **Min choices** — how few options the customer can pick.
    - **Max choices** — how many options the customer can pick (1 = radio, 2\+ = checkbox).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add options">
    Each option needs:

    - **Name** — what the customer sees (_Extra cheese_).
    - **Price** — additional charge, in ZMW. `0` for free options like _Well done_.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    The group is now available to attach to menu categories or individual menu items.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Attach a modifier group

On **Menu Categories**, pick a category and tick the modifier groups that should apply. Every menu item in that category will offer those modifiers automatically.

To attach a group to a single menu item only, use Menu Management → the item’s **Modifiers** section.

## Link a modifier option to an ingredient

This is the step that makes add-ons deduct stock and contribute to food cost.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Links tab">
    **Inventory → Modifiers → Links**. Every modifier option in your restaurant is listed on the left, grouped by group.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick an option">
    Tap the option you want to link (e.g. _Extra cheese_).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an ingredient link">
    Tap **Add Link**, search for the ingredient (e.g. `Cheddar cheese`), and enter a **deduct quantity** in the ingredient’s base unit — for example `0.03` kg.
  </Step>
  <Step title="(Optional) Multiple ingredients">
    A single option can consume more than one ingredient. _Add bacon_ could deduct `2` slices of bacon and `0.005` l of oil.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    From now on, every order line with this modifier auto-deducts the linked ingredients when the kitchen marks the item Ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The `deduct_quantity` is applied **per order line**, not per menu-item quantity. If a guest orders 2× burger with _Extra cheese_, the cheese ingredient is deducted 2× the deduct quantity.
</Note>

## How modifiers affect food cost

When a modifier is linked to an ingredient with a positive `deduct_quantity`, the modifier’s **cost** is `deduct_quantity × ingredient.unit_cost`. That cost is:

- Included in the food-cost calculation for the specific order line.
- Shown in the Menu Analytics report as add-on revenue vs add-on cost.

A modifier with a `0` deduct quantity has no cost impact — useful for preferences like _Well done_ or _No onions_.

## Removing a link

On the Links tab, tap the trash icon next to a linked ingredient. This does not delete the modifier or the ingredient — only the connection between them.

## Tips & best practices

<Tip>
  Link every priced add-on. If a modifier has a price, it almost certainly consumes an ingredient — catching those keeps food-cost reports accurate.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Use `0` deduct quantity for preferences that don’t change portion size (_Medium rare_, _No onions_). This keeps the option visible for the kitchen without polluting inventory.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  If two options share an ingredient (_Extra cheese_ and _Double cheese_), configure both links — don’t assume the customer will always pick the bigger one.
</Tip>

## Common mistakes

- **Modifier group attached but options unlinked** — the modifier sells, food cost looks great on paper, real stock disappears. Always link priced options.
- **Deduct quantity in the wrong unit** — `30` grams of cheese entered when the ingredient is in **kg** wipes out cheese stock in three orders.
- **Linking the _option name_ to a different ingredient** — _Add bacon_ linked to `Ham` because they were beside each other in the list. Double-check on save.
- **Forgetting to link _No X_ options** — they shouldn’t link (no consumption), but sometimes get accidentally set to `0.001`. Check the log.
